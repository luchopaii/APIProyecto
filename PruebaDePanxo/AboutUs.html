Chuck Norris

Who We Are

Chuck Norris (born March 10, 1940, in Ryan, Oklahoma, USA) is an American actor, martial artist, producer, and writer. He initially became famous as a world karate champion, gaining significant recognition during the 1960s and 1970s. Later, he rose to fame as an action movie star in films such as Invasion USA, Delta Force, and The Way of the Dragon, where he fought alongside Bruce Lee. In addition to his film career, he is widely known for his role in the successful television series Walker, Texas Ranger. Over time, his persona became a part of popular culture thanks to the famous “Chuck Norris facts,” exaggerated jokes about his strength and abilities. Off-screen, he is an active philanthropist and founder of several organizations for youth and veterans, and he has written several books on martial arts, fitness, and motivation.
